# DOST-SEI Undergraduate S&T Scholarship — Eligibility Rulebook (Illustrative)

> **Demo notice.** This is a **simplified, illustrative** rulebook authored for the
> Batayan demo. It is inspired by the structure of real Philippine S&T scholarships
> but the specific thresholds here are **not official** and must not be used to make
> real decisions. Batayan's job is to *reason over whatever rulebook it is given* and
> prove its verdict — not to be the source of truth.

## 1. Purpose

The Undergraduate Science & Technology (S&T) Scholarship supports academically
qualified, financially deserving Filipino students pursuing priority S&T degree
programs. Awards are competitive and subject to the eligibility rules below.

## 2. Citizenship and Residency

The applicant must be a natural-born Filipino citizen. Dual citizens who acquired
foreign citizenship after birth are evaluated under a separate track and are out of
scope for this rulebook.

## 3. Academic Merit

The applicant must have a General Weighted Average (GWA) of at least 90 out of 100
in the most recent completed school year. For this rulebook, GWA is expressed on a
0–100 percentage scale where a higher number is better.

## 4. Financial Need

The applicant's total annual household income must not exceed PHP 400,000. Household
income includes the combined gross income of the parents or legal guardians for the
preceding calendar year.

## 5. Priority Degree Programs

The applicant must be enrolled in, or admitted to, one of the priority S&T degree
programs: BS Computer Science, BS Information Technology, BS Civil Engineering,
BS Electronics Engineering, BS Biology, BS Chemistry, BS Mathematics, or BS Physics.
Programs outside this list are not covered by this scholarship.

## 6. Year Level

The scholarship is awarded to incoming first-year (year level 1) college students.
Students already in their second year or beyond are not eligible to begin the award
under this rulebook.

## 7. No Concurrent Government Scholarship

The applicant must not be holding any other government-funded scholarship at the same
time. Concurrent private or institutional grants are allowed and do not affect
eligibility under this rule.

## 8. Adjudication Notes

Each requirement above is a decisive rule. A confident "eligible" verdict requires
every decisive rule to be satisfied and individually cited. Where the applicant has
not supplied the information needed to check a rule, the decision is deferred rather
than assumed.
